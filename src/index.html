OAcheck

Turn OA policies into practice in minutes, not months

OAcheck analyzes papers covered by your Open Access policy to help you make them open.

What’s this?

Our open source tools, built in partnership with the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, will help you:

  • Discover

    papers covered by your policy

  • Check

    if papers comply

  • Simplify

    making papers more open for you and authors

Stay up to date

We’re working to make OAcheck available to funders, libraries, and authors. We’d love to keep you updated.

How OAcheck works

For you

  • Tell us what institution you’re from
  • We’ll provide you a custom analysis
  • You use it to make more papers open

The system

  • We discover your papers and data about them by searching open data, full text, landing pages, and your internal sources.
  • We analyse those papers against all the terms of your current policy and provide yes/no answers.
  • We’ll help you find and communicate how to make them more open.

Q&A

When will OAcheck be generally available?

We don’t know. OAcheck is already powering work at the Gates Foundation, and we’re working to make it generally available. If you’d like to help accelerate that, get in touch.

What will OACheck cost?

We don’t have details yet. As with all OA.Works tools, we plan to provide a free tier that will enable anyone to advance open access. We’ll also provide a paid for tier with flexible pricing for institutions who want more than we can provide freely.

What data does OACheck use?

OACheck will use all the sources listed on our infrastructure page, manual extraction where required, and your internal sources (e.g finance systems, previously generated lists etc).

We OACheck help even if we don’t have a policy, but still want to help our authors make their work open?

We’re not sure, but we have plans for something that could. Get in touch.

What type of policies will it cover?

Funder, university, and government Open Access policies globally.
